Iconic Runway Shows through Years

Christian Dior Couture primavera/verano 1998 Conde

Shortly after joining Christian Dior, John Galliano held an extravagant show at Paris's Opera Garnier, inspired by Marchesa Casati. The collection featured early 20th-century styles, including backless velvet gowns, mink-trimmed opera coats, lace sheaths, skirtsuits, and rose-adorned hats. The finale included a magical shower of pastel-colored butterfly-shaped confetti. Pure magic.

Jean Paul Gaultier at Paris Haute Couture 2021

Jean Paul Gaultier presented a collection that stood out for its creativity and originality. The collection was a tribute to his legacy in fashion, showcasing his characteristic bold and avant-garde style. With structured designs, intricate details, and a mix of materials and textures, Gaultier reaffirmed his reputation as an icon of haute couture. The runway featured everything from dramatic and theatrical pieces to more elegant and sophisticated looks, all with his distinctive artistic vision.

Alexander McQueen 2007 An Ode to Elegance

Alexander McQueen surprised with a collection where contemporary elegance merged with proposals more typical of the past century, inspired by Goya, Barry Lyndon, and Marchesa Casati.

Robert Wun's Bleeding Love Paris Couture Week Spring 2024.

Traditionally a white wedding gown is a canvas to celebrate the pureness of a bride’s virginity – “so frankly, it’s quite dated,” Wun quips. “And spending all those hours and hours embroidering is almost embracing the pain that comes with love. To fall in love is natural, but to accept the pain is not something that is easy.”
